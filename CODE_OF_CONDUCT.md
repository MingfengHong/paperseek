# Code of Conduct

PaperSeek is an open-source literature-search workflow for researchers and builders. We want the project to stay useful, rigorous, and welcoming to contributors with different backgrounds.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and constructive in issues, pull requests, reviews, and discussions.
- Focus criticism on ideas, evidence, code, documentation, and reproducible behavior.
- Assume good intent, but be clear when a change affects correctness, privacy, security, or research integrity.
- Keep API keys, private data, institutional credentials, and unpublished research materials out of public threads.
- Respect that PaperSeek outputs are research aids, not final evidence for a systematic review or formal claim.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, insults, threats, discrimination, or repeated personal attacks.
- Publishing or requesting secrets, credentials, private contact data, or protected content.
- Promoting paywall bypass, credential sharing, bulk protected PDF downloading, or other misuse.
- Deliberately submitting fabricated paper metadata, misleading benchmark claims, or unverifiable security reports.
- Repeatedly derailing maintainers or contributors after a decision has been explained.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove comments, close issues or pull requests, limit participation, or block users when needed to protect the project and its contributors. Enforcement decisions should be proportionate to the behavior and the risk to the community.
